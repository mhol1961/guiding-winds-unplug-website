Every Voyage Starts With a Feeling

The world is loud.
The water is not.

The week off the grid is a different kind of trip. You don't pick a hotel or thread a city. You pick a feeling — slow mornings on glass water, a snorkel into a reef no day-boat finds, a long lunch on deck with nowhere to be — and we build the week around it.

Eight cabins. Twelve guests max. A captain who's read these waters for a decade and a host who'll teach you to breathe again. Everything's in. Bring a swimsuit.

For three years we've quietly become the way people in the know go off-grid.

Our Story

Two captains.
One slow life.

Clint Kendall was born in Miami Beach and raised in the Florida Keys. He worked as a long-liner before he ever guided a guest — and he knows these waters the way most people know their own neighborhoods.

Dodie Kendall grew up between Fort Lauderdale and Pompano Beach. She runs the wellness side of every voyage — sunrise breathwork, sunset meditations, the long lunch you didn't know you needed.

Together they sail eight to twelve guests at a time. They cook. They host. They show up. That's the whole offer.
