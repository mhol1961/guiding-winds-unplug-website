The 2027
Voyages.

Three regions, fourteen weeks aboard. Each voyage is seven nights, all-inclusive, eight to twelve guests. The route flexes with weather and the room — these are starting points, not itineraries.

I

British Virgin Islands

Caribbean · 6 weeks · Jan – Mar & Nov – Dec · $3,550 / cabin

Norman Island. The Baths at Virgin Gorda before the day boats arrive. A downwind sail to Anegada for lobster on the beach. Jost Van Dyke at Foxy's on the loudest night of the trip. The classic chain, done at the pace it was meant to be done.

II

The Abacos & Marsh Harbour

Bahamas · 3 weeks · Mar 27 – May 8 · $3,550 / cabin

A shoulder-season run through the cays. Hope Town for the candy-striped lighthouse and a quiet anchorage. Man-O-War for the boat-building heritage. Green Turtle Cay for an afternoon swim with the rays. Pace: slower than the BVI.

III

Italy · Greece · Croatia

Mediterranean · 5 weeks · Jul – Oct · $3,850 / cabin

Two weeks along the Amalfi coast in July. A run through the Cyclades in August. Athens to the islands in September. The Dalmatian Coast in late September and early October. Hot afternoons, long lunches, swims in coves you'll wish were yours.

02 Aboard

Everything is in.
Bring a swimsuit.

The price is the price. Your cabin and head. Every meal, every drink. Fuel, dockage, every mooring ball. Snorkel gear, paddleboards, a daily wellness practice. A captain who has read these waters for a decade.

Not in: flights to embarkation port, crew gratuity, anything off the boat (excursions, dinners ashore, taxis), travel insurance.

01

Chef-Prepared Meals

Three meals a day, sourced locally wherever we anchor. Tell us your diet on the inquiry — we'll work with it.

02

Premium Bar

House wine, beer, classic cocktails. Sundowners on deck every night without fail.

03

Private Cabin & Head

Queen berth, your own bathroom with a real shower, AC at anchor, and a hatch with a view.

04

Snorkel Gear, Fins, SUPs

Masks for every guest. Two paddleboards on the back deck. Floaties for the lazy days.

05

Fuel, Dockage, Mooring

Every gallon of diesel, every marina fee, every Park Service ball. No add-ons at checkout.

06

Daily Wellness Practice

Optional sunrise breathwork at anchor. Sunset meditation. Guided journaling on a slow afternoon.

07

Captain & Host

Clint at the helm, Dodie hosting. You're never on watch unless you ask.

03 The Crew

Two captains.
One slow life.

Clint Kendall was born in Miami Beach and raised in the Florida Keys. The ocean was not a backdrop. It was a way of life. He worked as a long-liner before he ever guided a guest, and he knows these waters the way most people know their own neighborhoods.

Dodie Kendall grew up between Fort Lauderdale and Pompano Beach. She shares Clint's love for the sea — and adds something else: a quiet belief that the ocean is one of the most direct paths back to yourself. She runs the wellness side of every voyage. She is the reason "Unplug" is in the name.

They sail eight to twelve guests at a time. They cook. They host. They show up. That is the whole offer.

— Clint & Dodie KendallCaptains · Hosts · Permanent residents of the deck
