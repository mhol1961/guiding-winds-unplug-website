The Trip

Quiet mornings on glass water.
Long afternoons in places the day-trippers never reach.

We pick you up in Tortola and cast off the same afternoon. From there it's a week of running with the wind through the chain — anchoring at Norman Island for snorkel drifts through The Caves, climbing The Baths at sunrise before the day boats arrive, crossing to Anegada for lobster on the beach, and finishing the week at Jost Van Dyke with feet in the sand at the Soggy Dollar.

The route isn't fixed. We read the weather, we read the room, and we sail accordingly. Some weeks lean active — three snorkels a day, hikes ashore, paddleboarding at every anchorage. Some weeks lean still — long mornings with coffee on the bow, an hour of breathwork at sunrise, an early dinner under the stars. Most weeks are some of both.

Eight to twelve guests. Private cabins. No screen pressure. Clint handles the helm; Dodie handles everything else.

Itinerary

A week, day by day.

Sample route — actual itinerary flexes with weather and the group's pace. Every week ends at Jost Van Dyke.

Day 1 · Saturday

Embark at Tortola · Norman Island

Arrive at Beef Island, transfer to the boat at Nanny Cay. Welcome drinks, cabin walk-through, safety briefing. Short afternoon sail to The Bight at Norman Island. Snorkel The Caves before dinner. Sunset cocktails on deck.

Day 2 · Sunday

The Indians · The Baths · Spanish Town

Early sail to The Indians for the best snorkel of the trip. Continue to Virgin Gorda. Hike the granite boulder maze at The Baths in the late afternoon when the day boats have left. Dinner ashore in Spanish Town.

Day 3 · Monday

Anegada

A real downwind sail across the Anegada Passage. Drop the hook in Setting Point. Lobster on the beach at sunset — the BVI's most famous meal. Optional bonfire on Loblolly Bay.

Day 4 · Tuesday

Anegada · Marina Cay

Slow morning. Beach walk on Loblolly. Easy afternoon sail back south to Marina Cay. Pusser's rum punch on the hill at sunset.

Day 5 · Wednesday

Cooper Island · Salt Island

Quiet, snorkel-heavy day. Wreck dive over the RMS Rhone if the conditions cooperate. Cooper Island for dinner — sustainable bar and quiet anchorage.

Day 6 · Thursday

Jost Van Dyke · Sandy Cay · Foxy's

Across the Drake Channel to White Bay. Soggy Dollar Painkillers, lunch on the beach, optional swim to Sandy Cay. Dinner at Foxy's. The loudest night of the trip — embrace it.

Day 7 · Friday

Cane Garden Bay · Last Sunset

Short morning sail to Cane Garden Bay. Final guided breathwork session. Long lazy afternoon, last swim, captain's dinner aboard.

Day 8 · Saturday

Disembark · Tortola

Breakfast on deck. Return to Nanny Cay by 9am. Transfer to Beef Island for your flight home.

Before You Book

The questions guests actually ask.

Do I need to know how to sail?

No experience required. Clint runs the boat. If you want to help raise the sails or take a turn at the helm, he'll teach you. If you'd rather sit with a book, that's equally welcome.

What does the cabin look like?

Every cabin has a queen berth, your own private head with a shower, AC at anchor, and a hatch with a real view. No bunking. No shared baths.

What if I get seasick?

Catamarans are remarkably stable — most guests are fine. We anchor every night in protected water, so you're not sleeping in motion. We carry over-the-counter remedies; if you're prone, bring patches.

What's the cancellation policy?

A 25% deposit holds the cabin. Full refund if you cancel more than 90 days out, 50% between 90 and 45 days, no refund inside 45 days — but we'll happily rebook you to another week if availability allows. We strongly recommend travel insurance.

Can we bring kids?

Yes — confident swimmers age 8 and up are welcome on most weeks. If you're booking the whole boat as a family, any age. Just tell us when you inquire.

Is there WiFi?

Yes, there's a Starlink connection if you really need it. But the whole pitch of the boat is in the name. We strongly recommend keeping your phone in the cabin.
